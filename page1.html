Introduction

Medusa is a prominent figure in Greek mythology, known for her serpent hair and the ability to turn anyone who gazes upon her into stone.

Attributes

Medusa's most distinctive attribute is her hair made of snakes. She possesses a gaze that turns any living being into stone.

Her origins are traced to Gorgon, one of the three sisters with snakes for hair and the ability to petrify those who looked at them.

Stories and Legends

One of the most famous stories involving Medusa is the tale of Perseus, who was tasked with defeating her. With the help of the gods, Perseus managed to slay Medusa by avoiding direct eye contact and using a reflective shield to see her indirectly.

Medusa's head was later used as a weapon by Perseus, and it retained its petrifying power even after her death.
